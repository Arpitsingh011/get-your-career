Education of Science:-

BSc full form is Bachelor of Science. BSc can be further classified into two categories - BSc Honours and BSc General or Pass. The former focuses on one major subject area. The BSc subjects are designed to keep more focus on the Honours subject and also include subjects or papers from the elective subjects students opt for. The purpose of studying the BSc subjects is to inculcate theoretical, practical and research skills among students.On the other hand, a BSc provides basic knowledge of the major Science subjects to the students. The BSc subjects are slightly less rigorous, but it includes both theoretical and practical components. The pedagogy of BSc courses is a combination of theory and practical lessons. The BSc subjects include practical lessons which carry a considerable amount of weightage. To pass a semester, students need to pass both the theory as well practical exams. Subjects such as Physics, Chemistry, Biology and Mathematics form the core of the BSc. There are a plethora of specialisations and branches offered in the BSc courses.The 3-year undergraduate Bachelor of Science (BSc Full Form) degree is recognised across India. BSc includes research, calculation, and experimenting. Both Honours and General courses can be taken towards BSc. The distinction between a BSc Hons and a BSc General is that the former focuses on fundamental information about BSc while the latter combines electives and foundation courses. Candidates can look at the table below to learn more about the BSc courses.BSc or Bachelor of Science is an undergraduate degree of 3-year duration that is universally offered across institutes and universities across India. BSc is one of the most popular courses opted for by students who have a flair for scientific aptitude and zeal for research-oriented and calculative approaches based on a proven systematic method. BSc courses are all about experimentation, research and discovery. To get enrolled in BSc, candidates must make sure they have completed Class 12 in the Science stream with PCB/M (Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Mathematics) subjects. The popular entrance exams whose scores are accepted for BSc admission include CUET, ICAR AIEEA, JEE Main, CG PAT, MHT CET, KCET, etc. However, there are also some colleges which offer direct admission to BSc based on the Class 12 marks. The popular BSc colleges are Banaras Hindu University, Jamia Millia Islamia, Delhi University, Jawaharlal Nehru University, VIT Vellore, SOA University, etc. The average BSc course fees range between INR 20,000 and INR 2 Lakh.
